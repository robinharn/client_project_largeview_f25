Networking & Connections

What is Networking?

Networking is the process of establishing and cultivating relationships with others to exchange information, support, and opportunities. It often involves meeting new people - sometimes in your field of interest - and staying in touch with them over time.

What is a Connection?

A connection usually refers to the individual relationships you form through networking. Each connection is someone in your professional, academic, or personal circle with whom you can share information, ideas, and opportunities.

Key goals of networking

  • Finding internships or jobs
  • Learning about different careers or industries
  • Getting academic advice or research opportunities
  • Gaining mentorship or guidance
  • Collaborating on projects

Types of connections:

  • Professors, advisors, and university staff
  • Classmates, colleagues, or alumni
  • Professionals in your desired field
  • Community leaders or volunteers

Value of strong connections:

  • Access to insider knowledge or job leads
  • Potential for collaboration or mentorship
  • Personal support and inspiration
